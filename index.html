Family Frameworks — three directions for the podcast identity

A podcast about reading the family in front of you. Three wordmark & brand systems below, each built on one shared idea drawn straight from Legacy Stone’s own language — Build a Foundation & Framework. Every option carries a recurring “keystone block” mark and Legacy Stone’s grounded, natural-toned DNA, but each claims its own type, palette and voice.

What every option shares

The keystone block

A single mark built from stacked stone courses, with one block in the accent color — the “framework” made visible.

Grounded, natural tone

Muted, earthy palettes that sit beside Legacy Stone without copying it — nothing flashy, nothing loud.

Compassionate expertise

Confident and structural, but warm. Authority you can lean into, never aggressive or excitable.

Built to scale

Reads at a 60px Spotify tile, a YouTube thumbnail, and an email header. Wordmark, monogram and motif all flex.

01

Foundation

CLOSE COUSIN · GROUNDED & ARCHITECTURAL

The most direct heir to Legacy Stone. It keeps the parent brand’s condensed, all-caps voice (Oswald) and pairs it with a literal masonry mark — two words stacked like courses of stone, one set in ochre. Solid, plainspoken, built-to-last. The safest bridge if the client wants the lineage read instantly.

02

Keystone

MODERN & CLEAN · CONTEMPORARY PODCAST

The broadest-appeal option. A precise geometric sans (Space Grotesk) and a deep evergreen palette give it a current, premium podcast feel that still reads as part of the family. The framework idea becomes nested frames; the cover puts Bill front and center in a duotone portrait — the face of the show.

03

Frame

SIBLING · WARM & EDITORIAL

The most distinct of the three. A literary, high-contrast serif (Newsreader) and a warm clay-and-taupe palette give the show a pastoral, almost printed-essay quality — intimate conversations, set in type you’d find in a good book. The keystone becomes a quiet stacked-bar mark; the wordmark sits inside its own frame.

Where we’d go from here

01 / Direction

Client picks a lead direction (or a hybrid — e.g. Keystone’s palette with Foundation’s mark). We refine the single chosen wordmark to final.

02 / System build-out

Full episode-template kit, audiogram frames, Spotify/Apple tiles at final res, YouTube end cards, and a one-page usage sheet.

03 / Handoff

Packaged logo files (SVG/PNG), color + type tokens, and a short brand mini-guide that slots beside the Legacy Stone guidelines.
